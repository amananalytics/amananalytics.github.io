About Me

I'm Abdulrahman Sanni, a final-year Statistics student at the University of Abuja. My academic journey has been built around mastering the mathematics of uncertainty — probability distributions, stochastic modeling, and the statistical frameworks that underlie real-world decision-making.

My final year project investigates job creation and separation dynamics in Nigerian businesses, drawing on the 2024 NBS Job Creation Survey. I apply econometric and comparative statistical methods to extract policy-relevant insights from one of Nigeria's most comprehensive labour market datasets.

"Statistics is not about numbers — it's about making good decisions under uncertainty."

Beyond the classroom, I'm passionate about building things with data — from dashboards and models to explorations in the Web3/data ecosystem. I'm actively seeking opportunities in data analysis and quantitative research after graduation.
